About

Ph.D. Student in GeoSpatial Information Science

My current research lines are in the area of spatial trajectories analysis, vehicle movement analysis, and developing AI methods for smart transportation.

Hi, My name is Reza. I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Geomatics Engineering, University of Calgary, Canada. I am currently working at the Intelligent Geospatial Data Mining Lab (IGDM) under the supervision and advisory of Prof. Xin Wang. I hold a master degree in Geospatial Information Systems (GIS) from the K.N.Toosi University of Technology and a bachelor of science in Geomatics Engineering from Iran University of Science and Technology (IUST). The focus of my current research as a Ph.D. student is on developing Artificial Intelligence (deep learning) methods for the improvement of smart transportation systems under extreme weather conditions.

I have also spent over three years working at FANAP ICT as a senior spatial data analyst. Working within diverse industrial and academic environments helped me developing my technical and analytical expertise as well as academic skills. I took the opportunities to expand my knowledge of a range of data science methods and techniques, including and deep learning approaches (e.g. RNNs, GCNs, etc.), as well as various programming languages (such as Python, Java and Matlab).

If you want to know more about the projects I have done, contact me, check my Github, and see my Vitae.
